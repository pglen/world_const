## 10. Rewrote Article 1 in Chapter III - Human Participation
**FROM:**
```html
<h3>Article 1: Citizenship and Political Participation</h3>
<p><strong>Citizen Definition:</strong> A citizen is defined as a person born in a particular jurisdiction, or bestowed with citizenship by that jurisdiction. For the purpose of this Constitution, the distinction between a citizen and non-citizen is as follows:</p>

<p><strong>Equal Rights Principle:</strong> All legal rights are identical; all rights are human rights. All considerations are identical regardless of citizenship status.</p>

<p><strong>Affected-Party Voting:</strong> Elections and political powers are given on a basis that if one is affected by the matter, one has a say in that matter. The affected threshold is established by a six-month presence in a particular locale, and/or more than half-year documented presence per annum. This ensures that all those who live and work in a jurisdiction have meaningful participation in its governance.</p>
```

**TO:**
```html
<h3>Article 1: Human Participation and Political Inclusion</h3>
<p><strong>Universal Participation:</strong> Every human being present within any jurisdiction for more than six months in any calendar year shall have the right to participate in that jurisdiction's governance. Political power flows to those affected by decisions - the affected-party principle ensures that all who live, work, and contribute to a society have meaningful voice in its governance.</p>

<p><strong>Equal Standing:</strong> All humans possess equal moral worth and equal rights regardless of nationality, origin, migration status, or any other characteristic. The measure of participation is presence and contribution, not accident of birth.</p>
```
