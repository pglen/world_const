Chapter IV: Global Economic Framework

Article 13: Planetary Resource Management

Natural Resources of Global Significance including atmosphere, deep seabed, biodiversity hotspots, and polar regions shall be treated as shared commons. Global Resource Fees shall be imposed on carbon emissions, deep-sea mining, data extraction, and commons usage to fund the Planetary Resource Dividend.

Article 14: Progressive Global Taxation

A progressive global taxation system shall address wealth inequality and fund universal social services. Natural resource extraction and pollution shall bear external costs. Financial transactions and wealth accumulation shall be progressively taxed to prevent extreme inequality.

Article 15: Fair Trade and Development

International trade shall promote sustainable development, reduce inequality, and respect environmental and labor standards. Trade privileges shall be tied to transparency, democratic reforms, and human rights compliance. Special consideration and support shall be provided to developing nations.

Article 15A: Free Enterprise and Equal Opportunity

Free enterprise shall be supported with no discrimination, no preferences, and equal opportunity for all. No human being or enterprise should experience fear or favor in economic activities. The economic framework shall ensure fair competition and prevent monopolistic practices while supporting innovation and entrepreneurship.

Article 16: Innovation and Knowledge Commons

Open innovation and knowledge sharing shall be promoted for education and key technologies. Research and development in critical areas shall be globally coordinated. Equitable benefit-sharing shall ensure technological advances serve all humanity.
