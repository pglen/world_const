Chapter II: Three-Tiered Governance Structure

Article 4: Global Governance

United Earth Assembly (UEA) shall be the primary legislative body with:

  • Proportional representation by population
  • Additional seats for indigenous and marginalized communities
  • Mixed representation combining national delegates with randomly selected citizens
  • No single executive authority or veto power ("No King, No Veto" principle)
  • Leadership through rotating facilitation rather than permanent hierarchy
  • Governance by consent, being conservative in output and liberal in acceptance of diverse perspectives
  • Recognition that force is infeasible in modern governance, requiring agreement of all parties involved

Article 5: Regional Governance

Continental Federations shall coordinate regional development, harmonize policies, and provide intermediate governance between global and local levels. These shall include African, European, Asian-Pacific, American, and other regional groupings determined by geographic and cultural affinities.

Article 6: Local and National Governance

Sovereign Local Governments shall preserve language, religion, culture, and legal traditions while operating within the universal rights framework. Nations shall maintain autonomy over internal affairs not requiring global cooperation.

Article 7: Executive and Administrative Bodies

Global Council shall implement Assembly decisions with rotating regional representation and no permanent executive authority. Following the principle of collective leadership, there shall be no presidents or single executive heads - instead, governance is conducted through a council of equals, similar to a round table of knights, recognizing that no single individual has the capacity to fulfill the challenges of global leadership.

Age Limit for Leadership: Recognizing that human faculties naturally degrade towards the end of life, no person shall be elected to any governing body at or above the age of 60, with the global mean retirement age established at 64. This ensures effective governance while preserving the wisdom of elders through advisory positions for those beyond office-holding age.

Military Service Restriction: No person with military background or combat command experience shall be permitted to hold leadership or governing positions, recognizing the need for civilian governance. Only personnel deployed in non-combatant or supporting roles are eligible for elected office.

Specialized Agencies shall include:

  • Public Health Agency
  • Environmental Protection Agency
  • Science and Innovation Agency
  • Social Development Agency
  • Digital Governance Agency
  • Disaster Response Agency
  • Ethics Oversight Council (12 independent members with rotating facilitation)

Article 8: Judiciary and Enforcement

World Court and Planetary Court shall adjudicate disputes, ensure compliance, and prosecute crimes against humanity, war crimes, and ecocide. Global Anti-Corruption Tribunal shall handle cross-border corruption cases.
