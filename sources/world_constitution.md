# World Constitution

## Preamble: Grassroots Foundation

**Grassroots Implementation Method**: This document has unlikely support in current power structure. The main method of this document to find footing is a grassroots movement. This movement may originate from the lowest level of power: the people. If everyone on the planet makes the decision to want fair governance, to want no wars, to want no dictators, the current ruling entities have no choice, but to adopt the principles outlined in this document.

**Core Foundation**: Diversity as Ultimate Strength. Despite all other indicators, diversity is the ultimate strength. In nature, all things happen every time, everywhere at once. Survival of anything or anyone is an event bordering a miracle. Diversity is a phenomenon that allows or even promotes survival or thriving of any entity.

We, the people of Earth, recognizing our shared humanity, common destiny, and sacred responsibility to present and future generations, hereby establish this World Constitution through grassroots movement to promote peace without wars, justice without presidents, equality through diversity, and democratic participation without military leadership. 

**Government Prototype Principle**: The global government described in this constitution serves as a prototype for successive levels of governing bodies. This serves as a safety valve for local governance, preventing corruption, abuse and runaway control of resources and people.

We affirm that sovereignty is shared responsibility, that Earth's resources are our common heritage, and that all humans possess inherent and inalienable rights, with particular emphasis on youth as our most valuable resource and diversity as our ultimate strength.

## Chapter III: No More Wars - Complete Demilitarization

### Article 10: No More Wars Resolution
**Peace Principle**: This resolution adapts the principle of peace on planet. To that effect, demilitarization starts immediately.

### Article 11: Immediate Demilitarization Steps
**Step 1 - Foreign Withdrawal**: Withdrawing every foreign military personnel and equipment from every country.

**Step 2 - Military Reduction**: Unwinding military to a level proportional to territory and/or population, to a level matching agreed international norms.

**Step 3 - Conflict Suppression Only**: Availing local military to conflict suppression. The suppression mechanism can only be invoked by the international body, and only in case of ongoing conflict.

**Step 4 - No More Wars**: No more wars. Only negotiated outcomes.

### Article 12: Military Personnel Restrictions
**No Military Leadership**: For leadership and/or governing positions, no person shall be permitted to hold a role if that person has military background or military involvement. Person holding any rank, commanding in any capacity is disqualified. The exception to this rule is personnel deployed in a non-combating or supporting role.

### Article 13: Peaceful Resolution Only
**Negotiated Outcomes Only**: All international disputes shall be resolved through negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or judicial settlement before any enforcement measures. Only negotiated outcomes shall be considered legitimate.

## Chapter II: Government Prototype and Structure

### Article 5: Government Prototype Framework
**Prototype Principle**: The global government described in this constitution serves as a prototype for the successive levels of governing bodies. This serves as a safety valve for local governance, preventing corruption, abuse and runaway control of resources and people.

### Article 6: Four-Tiered Governmental Structure
**Structure Design**: The structure is laid out as follows: Four tiered governmental structure: Executive, Legislative, Monitoring and Judicial.

**Executive Branch**: Implements Assembly decisions through collective leadership. No presidents. The post is replaced with a round table of decision makers. The number of decision makers shall be an odd number, no less than five, no more than nine. Elections at regular intervals, if necessary with supervision from a higher level of governing body, ultimately supervision by the international governing body.

**Legislative Branch**: Consists of representative assemblies with proportional representation and citizen participation.

**Monitoring Branch**: Independent oversight mechanism to prevent corruption, abuse, and ensure transparency across all government operations.

**Judicial Branch**: Interprets laws and adjudicates disputes through court systems, with mechanisms for appeal to higher levels.

### Article 7: No Presidents Principle
**Collective Leadership**: No presidents. No single individual has the capacity to fulfill that challenge. The president is replaced by a team of individuals, similar to a round table of knights. The post of the president is replaced with multiple decision makers. The number of decision makers shall be an odd number, no less than five, no more than nine. The members of this group should be selected by elections at regular intervals.

### Article 8: Inter-Branch Balance and Dispute Resolution
**Checks and Balances**: The four branches act as a check on each other to create a balanced decision making environment.

**Dispute Resolution Mechanism**: Mechanism should exist for dispute resolution in the form of elevation to the next higher level body.

### Article 9: Global Governance Assembly
**United Earth Assembly**: The primary legislative body with proportional representation, rotating facilitation, and no veto power.

**Regional and Local Governance**: Continental federations coordinate regional development while sovereign local governments preserve language, religion, culture, and legal traditions.

## Chapter IV: Leadership Qualifications and Restrictions

### Article 14: Age Limit Principle
**Natural Life Cycle Recognition**: Age limit. A human being has a limited lifespan. Towards the end of life, the faculties degrade. This is a natural process, there is no acknowledgement or shame is needed. For this reason, an age limit is imposed on leadership.

**Global Retirement Standard**: This age is allocated to the global mean retirement age of sixty four (64).

**Election Restrictions**: No person should be elected to any governing body at or above this limit, minus the length of term for that particular office. For instance, a governing position for a four year term cannot elect any person at or above the age of 60.

**Preservation of Wisdom**: This is a necessary arrangement, but the wisdom of elders are not lost, as people who are not eligible for office can still hold advisory positions.

### Article 15: Cultural Evolution and Qualifications
**Qualified Leadership Priority**: Cultural evolution. A knowledgeable/qualified person has a severe disadvantage against a person with a charismatic presence. Even if the latter has lesser knowledge or lower qualifications. In many instances the qualified person has a disadvantage even if the charismatic opponent has no relevant qualification(s).

**Evolution Mandate**: In the cultural evolution we have to morph into a state of mind where qualifications are ahead of any other trait.

### Article 16: Equal Opportunity Economy
**Free Enterprise Support**: Economy. Free enterprise should be supported with no discrimination, no preferences and equal opportunity for all. No human being, no enterprise should experience fear of favor.

### Article 17: Modernization Adaptation
**Innovation Integration**: Modernization. In the age of global communications, artificial intelligence one has to acknowledge and perhaps adapt to the new innovations.

### Article 8: Judiciary and Enforcement
**World Court and Planetary Court** shall adjudicate disputes, ensure compliance, and prosecute crimes against humanity, war crimes, and ecocide. **Global Anti-Corruption Tribunal** shall handle cross-border corruption cases.

## Chapter IV: Global Economic Framework

### Article 13: Planetary Resource Management
**Natural Resources of Global Significance** including atmosphere, deep seabed, biodiversity hotspots, and polar regions shall be treated as shared commons. **Global Resource Fees** shall be imposed on carbon emissions, deep-sea mining, data extraction, and commons usage to fund the Planetary Resource Dividend.

### Article 14: Progressive Global Taxation
A progressive global taxation system shall address wealth inequality and fund universal social services. Natural resource extraction and pollution shall bear external costs. Financial transactions and wealth accumulation shall be progressively taxed to prevent extreme inequality.

### Article 15: Fair Trade and Development
International trade shall promote sustainable development, reduce inequality, and respect environmental and labor standards. Trade privileges shall be tied to transparency, democratic reforms, and human rights compliance. Special consideration and support shall be provided to developing nations.

### Article 16: Innovation and Knowledge Commons
Open innovation and knowledge sharing shall be promoted for education and key technologies. Research and development in critical areas shall be globally coordinated. Equitable benefit-sharing shall ensure technological advances serve all humanity.

## Chapter V: Environmental Protection and Climate Justice

### Article 17: Climate and Environmental Framework
**Mandatory net-zero greenhouse gas emissions** shall be achieved by scientifically determined targets. **Global reserve zones** shall protect endangered ecosystems. **Ecocide and large-scale environmental harm** shall be globally prosecutable offenses.

### Article 18: Biodiversity and Ecosystem Protection
Conservation of biodiversity and sustainable resource use shall be mandatory. International environmental agents shall enforce protection measures. Planetary boundaries framework shall be integrated with human rights obligations.

### Article 19: Intergenerational Responsibility
All governance decisions shall consider impacts on future generations. A duty to maintain planetary integrity shall be legally binding. Global environmental monitoring and scientific coordination shall guide policy.

## Chapter VI: Anti-Corruption and Transparency Framework

### Article 20: Citizens' Integrity Chamber
**Citizens' Integrity Chamber (CIC)** shall be a rotating, lottery-selected citizen panel reviewing high-level decisions. Members shall serve limited terms with comprehensive protection and resources.

### Article 21: Radical Transparency
Mandatory publication of all government contracts, budgets, meeting minutes, and lobbying interactions shall be required. **Public Ledger Systems** using immutable cryptographic logs shall track finances, decision logic, and asset declarations.

### Article 22: Whistleblower Protection
Anonymous reporting channels with legal immunity and financial rewards shall be established. Whistleblowers shall receive comprehensive protection and support.

### Article 23: Media and Investigative Support
**Global Investigative Journalism Trust** with firewalled funding and rotating editorial oversight by civil society boards shall be established. Incentives for cross-border investigative collaborations shall be provided.

### Article 24: AI Oversight and Ethics
Open-source, auditable algorithms with independent AI Ethics Ombudsman shall govern automated systems. Algorithmic decisions shall be explainable and contestable.

## Chapter IX: Dispute Resolution and Enforcement

### Article 32: Peaceful Resolution
All international disputes shall be resolved through negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or judicial settlement before any enforcement measures.

### Article 33: Enforcement Mechanisms
The World Assembly may authorize graduated measures including diplomatic pressure, economic sanctions, targeted measures against individuals, and peacekeeping operations to ensure compliance with this Constitution.

### Article 34: Global Peacekeeping Service
A volunteer-based Global Peacekeeping Service shall intervene in cases of genocide, war crimes, crimes against humanity, and ecocide with clear mandates and civilian protection priorities.

## Chapter X: Constitutional Flexibility and Development

### Article 35: Amendment Process
This Constitution may be amended by:
- Two-thirds majority of the World Assembly
- Citizen referendum with ≥60% turnout and ≥66% approval
- Ratification by three-fourths of member nations through democratic processes

### Article 36: Review and Evolution
Every five years, a **World Constitutional Convention** shall review effectiveness and recommend improvements. Every ten years, a comprehensive review shall consider fundamental restructuring if needed.

### Article 37: Emergency Powers
During global emergencies, limited temporary powers may be granted with strict time limits, judicial oversight, and citizen approval through referenda within 90 days.

## Chapter XI: Implementation and Transition

### Article 38: Transitional Implementation
A **Foundational Charter** with pilot federations shall operate independently initially, similar to existing international organizations (WHO, ICC, ILO). UN Parliamentary Assembly shall evolve toward bicameral global legislation.

### Article 39: Incentives and Support
Financial and technical incentives shall encourage developing nations' participation. Early adopter benefits shall include:
- Innovation grants and sustainable finance
- Technology transfer and capacity building
- Preferential trade and investment access
- Priority access to global resources and programs

### Article 40: Ratification Process
This Constitution shall enter into force when ratified by two-thirds of nations representing at least two-thirds of the world's population through both governmental approval and citizen referenda.

### Article 41: Supremacy and Compatibility
This Constitution shall be the supreme law of the world. National laws inconsistent with its provisions shall be invalidated to the extent of inconsistency. Cultural and religious practices shall be respected unless they violate fundamental human rights.

## Chapter XII: Final Provisions

### Article 42: Universal Jurisdiction
Crimes against humanity, genocide, war crimes, torture, slavery, and ecocide shall be subject to universal jurisdiction regardless of where committed or by whom.

### Article 43: Scientific Cooperation
Global coordination of scientific research for pandemics, climate response, energy transitions, and other existential challenges shall be mandatory. Open-access publishing and equitable benefit-sharing shall be required.

### Article 44: Symbolic Elements
The world shall adopt symbols representing unity in diversity, including a flag, anthem, and celebration of World Citizenship Day to promote global identity while respecting local cultures.
