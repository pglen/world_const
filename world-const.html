Preamble

We, the people of Earth, recognizing our shared humanity and responsibility to present and future generations, establish this World Constitution to promote peace, justice, equality, environmental sustainability, democratic participation, and shared prosperity. We affirm that sovereignty is shared responsibility, Earth's resources are our common heritage, and all humans possess inherent and inalienable rights.

Grassroots Foundation: This Constitution recognizes that meaningful global governance emerges through grassroots movement from the people themselves. When citizens demand fair governance, reject war, and oppose dictatorship, existing power structures will be compelled to adopt these principles through peaceful, democratic means.

Fundamental Principles

Government Prototype Principle

The global government serves as a prototype for successive levels of governing bodies, providing safety valves for local governance to prevent corruption, abuse, and runaway control of resources and people.

Diversity as Strength

Diversity is the ultimate strength and primary mechanism for human survival and thriving. All structures shall promote and protect diversity in all forms - biological, cultural, linguistic, cognitive, and gender diversity. Integration is the preferred approach: do not promote or suppress, allow all to integrate as one human being.

Coexistence Principle

Be conservative in output and liberal in acceptance. In modern governance, force is infeasible; decisions must be made by consent with agreement of all parties involved.

Youth Priority

Youth are our most valuable resource. Promote scientific education, coexistence, and participation in the greater good. No religious education for minors; where separation is impossible, the secular nature must be explicitly clear.

Shared Sovereignty and Subsidiarity

Sovereignty is shared responsibility for the common good. Nations retain local authority while committing to global cooperation. Decisions are made at the lowest competent level; no single entity has veto power over global decisions.

Peace Without Wars

No more wars - only negotiated outcomes. Immediate demilitarization begins: withdraw foreign military personnel, reduce military to proportional levels, and avail local military for conflict suppression only when invoked by international body. No person with military background shall hold leadership roles.

Global Communication

English is the primary language for international affairs and inter-area communications. This applies only to communications between different linguistic regions. All local languages are protected and preserved as cultural heritage.

Religious Freedom and Secular Governance

Every person has the right to practice their religion individually and in community. Global governance is secular - no religion influences official world affairs. No promotion or suppression of religion through state mechanisms. The state serves as neutral mediator in religious disputes.

Government Structure

Four-Tiered System

Executive Branch: Collective leadership through a round table of 5-9 decision makers elected at regular intervals. No single president - governance by team.

Legislative Branch: United Earth Assembly with proportional representation by population, additional seats for indigenous communities, and mixed representation combining national delegates with randomly selected citizens.

Monitoring Branch: Independent oversight preventing corruption and ensuring transparency across all government operations.

Judicial Branch: World Court system interpreting laws and adjudicating disputes with appeal mechanisms to higher levels.

Leadership Qualifications

  • No single president - collective leadership only
  • Age limit: No election for positions where age plus term length exceeds 64 years
  • Military restriction: No persons with military background may hold leadership roles
  • Qualifications优先: Cultural evolution toward prioritizing expertise over charisma

Regional and Local Governance

Continental federations coordinate regional development. Local governments preserve language, religion, culture, and legal traditions within universal rights framework. Decisions made at lowest competent level (subsidiarity principle).

Specialized Agencies

Public Health, Environmental Protection, Science and Innovation, Social Development, Digital Governance, Disaster Response, and Ethics Oversight Council.

Judiciary and Enforcement

World Court adjudicates disputes and prosecutes crimes against humanity, war crimes, and ecocide. Global Anti-Corruption Tribunal handles cross-border corruption cases.

Rights and Freedoms

Civil and Political Rights

  • Freedom of speech, expression, and peaceful assembly
  • Right to privacy, personal autonomy, and data protection
  • Freedom of thought, conscience, religion, and belief
  • Right to participate in government through free, fair elections
  • Due process and fair trial rights
  • Right to access information and government transparency

Economic and Social Rights

  • Right to adequate standard of living (food, housing, healthcare)
  • Planetary Resource Dividend providing universal basic income
  • Universal, inclusive, multilingual education at all levels
  • Right to work, fair labor conditions, workplace democracy
  • Social security protection in old age, disability, hardship
  • Equal access to opportunity and basic needs for all

Environmental Rights

  • Right to clean, healthy, ecologically balanced environment
  • Right to clean water, clean air, and healthy food
  • Right to participate in environmental decision-making
  • Right of future generations to planetary integrity and biodiversity
  • Protection from ecocide and large-scale environmental harm

Digital and Technology Rights

  • Right to internet access and digital inclusion
  • Right to digital privacy and protection from surveillance
  • Right to open-source knowledge and technological innovation
  • Right to algorithmic transparency and accountability
  • Protection from AI bias and automated decision-making harms

Economic Framework

Planetary Resource Management

Key natural resources (atmosphere, deep seabed, biodiversity, polar regions) are shared commons. Global Resource Fees on carbon emissions, deep-sea mining, data extraction fund the Planetary Resource Dividend, ensuring all humanity benefits from shared planetary resources.

Progressive Global Taxation

Progressive taxation addresses wealth inequality and funds universal social services. Natural resource extraction and pollution bear external costs. Financial transactions and wealth accumulation are progressively taxed to prevent extreme inequality.

Fair Trade and Development

International trade promotes sustainable development, reduces inequality, and respects environmental and labor standards. Trade privileges tied to transparency, democratic reforms, and human rights compliance. Developing nations receive special consideration and support.

Free Enterprise and Equal Opportunity

Free enterprise supported with no discrimination, no preferences, equal opportunity for all. No fear or favor in economic activities - fair competition guaranteed for all participants.

Innovation and Knowledge Commons

Open innovation and knowledge sharing promoted for education and key technologies. Research and development in critical areas globally coordinated. Equitable benefit-sharing ensures technological advances serve all humanity.

Environmental Protection

Climate and Environmental Framework

  • Mandatory net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by scientific targets
  • Global reserve zones protecting endangered ecosystems
  • Ecocide and large-scale environmental harm as prosecutable offenses

Biodiversity and Ecosystem Protection

  • Mandatory conservation of biodiversity and sustainable resource use
  • International environmental agents enforcing protection measures
  • Planetary boundaries framework integrated with human rights

Intergenerational Responsibility

  • All governance decisions consider impacts on future generations
  • Legally binding duty to maintain planetary integrity
  • Global environmental monitoring and scientific coordination guide policy

Implementation

Anti-Corruption and Transparency

  • Citizens' Integrity Chamber: Lottery-selected citizen panel reviewing high-level decisions
  • Radical Transparency: Mandatory publication of all government contracts, budgets, decisions
  • Whistleblower Protection: Anonymous reporting with legal immunity and financial rewards
  • Investigative Support: Global Investigative Journalism Trust with protected funding
  • AI Oversight: Open-source, auditable algorithms with independent ethics oversight

Participatory Democracy

  • Global Referenda: Direct citizen votes on key issues (≥60% turnout, ≥66% approval)
  • Citizen Juries: Randomly selected citizens providing deliberative guidance
  • Hybrid Conventions: National delegates combined with randomly selected citizens
  • Ethics Veto: Citizen mechanisms to block unjust decisions and remove corrupt officials
  • Integrity Education: Mandatory ethics training and civic education programs

Education, Culture, and Knowledge

  • Universal Education: Inclusive, multilingual education at all levels promoting peace and critical thinking
  • Secular Education for Minors: No religious education under adulthood age
  • Cultural Diversity: Heritage preservation, intercultural exchange, endangered language protection
  • Global Knowledge Systems: Open-access scientific knowledge integrating traditional wisdom

Dispute Resolution and Enforcement

  • Peaceful Resolution: All disputes resolved through negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or judicial settlement
  • Graduated Enforcement: Diplomatic pressure, economic sanctions, targeted measures, peacekeeping operations
  • Global Peacekeeping Service: Volunteer-based intervention for genocide, war crimes, ecocide

Constitutional Flexibility

  • Amendment Process: Requires legislative supermajority and citizen referendum
  • Regular Review: World Constitutional Convention every 5 years, comprehensive review every 10 years
  • Emergency Powers: Limited temporary powers with strict oversight and citizen approval

Transition and Ratification

  • Foundational Charter: Pilot federations operating independently initially
  • Incentives and Support: Financial and technical incentives for developing nations
  • Ratification: Two-thirds of nations representing two-thirds of world population
  • Grassroots Foundation: Popular support drives adoption through peaceful, democratic means
  • Supremacy: Constitution is supreme law; national laws inconsistent with it are invalidated

Final Provisions

  • Universal Jurisdiction: Crimes against humanity, genocide, war crimes, torture, slavery, ecocide
  • Scientific Cooperation: Global coordination for pandemics, climate, energy transitions
  • Symbolic Elements: Flag, anthem, World Citizenship Day promoting unity in diversity

Conclusion

Adopted by the peoples of the world, united in commitment to peace, justice, environmental sustainability, democratic participation, and shared prosperity. In our diversity lies our strength, in our cooperation lies our security, and in our shared humanity lies our hope.

Appendices

Glossary

Key terms and concepts used in the World Constitution, defined for clarity and understanding.

A

Algorithmic Transparency - The requirement that automated decision-making systems must be explainable and contestable, with humans able to understand how decisions are reached.

Assembly - The United Earth Assembly (UEA), primary legislative body representing all people of Earth.

B

Bicameral - Having two legislative chambers; in this context, referring to evolution toward a two-house global legislature.

Biodiversity Hotspots - Geographic areas with significant levels of biodiversity that are threatened by human activities.

C

Citizen Jury - A group of 100-200 randomly selected citizens who deliberate on specific policy issues and provide recommendations.

Citizens' Integrity Chamber (CIC) - A rotating panel of lottery-selected citizens who review high-level government decisions for integrity and public interest compliance.

Collective Rights - Rights held by groups, particularly indigenous communities, to preserve their heritage, language, traditions, and territories.

Commons - Natural resources and systems that are shared by all humanity rather than owned by individuals or corporations.

Continental Federations - Regional groupings of nations for coordination and governance at the continental level.

D

Deliberative Democracy - A form of democracy that emphasizes deliberation, reflection, and reason-giving in decision-making processes.

Digital Deliberation Platforms - Online systems that enable citizens to discuss, debate, and collaborate on policy issues.

Digital Inclusion - Ensuring that all people have access to and can effectively use digital technologies and internet services.

E

Ecocide - Large-scale environmental destruction that causes widespread or long-term damage to ecosystems.

Environmental Rights - Legal protections ensuring access to clean water, air, food, and a healthy environment.

Ethics Veto - Citizen-initiated mechanisms to block government decisions that violate ethical principles or public interest.

External Costs - Negative impacts of economic activities that are not reflected in market prices, such as pollution or resource depletion.

F

Foundational Charter - The initial operating agreement that establishes pilot programs and basic structures for the World Constitution.

Future Generations - People who will live after current generations, whose rights and interests must be protected in current decision-making.

G

Global Anti-Corruption Tribunal - A judicial body that handles cross-border corruption cases and ensures international compliance with anti-corruption standards.

Global Commons - Shared resources and systems that belong to all humanity, including atmosphere, oceans, biodiversity, and polar regions.

Global Council - The executive body that implements Assembly decisions with rotating regional representation.

Global Investigative Journalism Trust - An independent organization with protected funding that supports cross-border investigative reporting.

Global Peacekeeping Service - A volunteer-based force that intervenes in cases of genocide, war crimes, crimes against humanity, and ecocide.

Global Referenda - Direct votes by all citizens on major policy questions, requiring significant participation and approval thresholds.

Governance - The systems and processes by which decisions are made and implemented in society.

H

Hybrid Conventions - Meetings that combine national delegates with randomly selected citizens to address major policy questions.

I

Inalienable Rights - Fundamental rights that cannot be taken away, suspended, or limited under any circumstances.

Innovation Grants - Funding provided to support new technologies, approaches, and solutions to global challenges.

Intergenerational Responsibility - The obligation to consider and protect the interests of future generations in current decision-making.

L

Localized Autonomy - The right of local communities and nations to govern their internal affairs without external interference.

O

Open-Source - Software, algorithms, or knowledge that is freely available for anyone to use, modify, and distribute.

Open-Access - Scientific research and publications that are available to everyone without financial barriers.

P

Participatory Democracy - A system where citizens actively participate in decision-making processes beyond just voting.

Participatory Democracy Mechanisms - Tools and processes that enable direct citizen involvement in governance.

Peaceful Resolution - The requirement that all international disputes must be resolved through negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or judicial settlement.

Planetary Boundaries - Scientific limits within which humanity can safely operate to avoid catastrophic environmental changes.

Planetary Resource Dividend - Universal basic income or services funded by fees on the use of global commons resources.

Popular Impeachment - Citizen-initiated processes for removing corrupt or ineffective officials from office.

Progressive Global Taxation - A tax system where rates increase with ability to pay, applied globally to reduce inequality.

Public Ledger Systems - Immutable cryptographic records that track government finances, decisions, and asset declarations.

R

Radical Transparency - The principle that all government operations, contracts, and decisions must be publicly accessible and understandable.

Referenda - Direct votes by citizens on specific policy questions or constitutional amendments.

Resource Fees - Charges imposed on the use or extraction of global commons resources.

Rotating Facilitation - Leadership systems where power and responsibilities rotate among participants rather than being held permanently by individuals or groups.

S

Shared Sovereignty - A redefinition of sovereignty as shared responsibility for the common good rather than absolute national authority.

Specialized Agencies - Organizations focused on specific areas like health, environment, science, social development, etc.

Subsidiarity - The principle that decisions should be made at the lowest competent level closest to affected people.

Sustainable Development - Development that meets present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.

T

Technology Transfer - The sharing of technologies, knowledge, and capabilities between nations or organizations.

Transitional Implementation - The process of moving from current systems to the new World Constitution framework over time.

Treaties - Formal agreements between nations that establish rights, obligations, and cooperation mechanisms.

U

United Earth Assembly (UEA) - The primary global legislative body representing all people of Earth.

Universal Jurisdiction - The principle that certain crimes (like genocide, war crimes) can be prosecuted anywhere regardless of where they were committed.

Universal Rights - Rights that apply equally to all human beings regardless of nationality, culture, or other characteristics.

Unconditional Rights - Rights that cannot be suspended or limited under any circumstances, even during emergencies.

V

Veto Power - The ability of a single entity to block decisions; specifically prohibited in the World Constitution system.

Virtual Reality Democracy - Technology-enabled systems that allow citizens to participate in governance regardless of geographic location.

Whistleblower Protection - Legal safeguards and support systems for people who report corruption, illegal activities, or violations of public trust.

World Assembly - See United Earth Assembly (UEA).

World Court - The global judicial body that adjudicates international disputes and ensures compliance with constitutional provisions.

World Constitutional Convention - Regular gatherings to review the constitution's effectiveness and recommend improvements.

This glossary is intended to help citizens understand and engage with the World Constitution. For additional clarification or discussion of specific terms, please refer to the relevant sections of the constitution or contact your local citizen assembly.
