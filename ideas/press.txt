Free press. The establishments of the FREE PRESS is an inalienable right.
Free press is defined by a no intervention policy. Nor encouragement, no
hindrance of the flow of information is permitted. All information
is to be tagged - at minimum - with date, source and publisher. Machine
generated content should me marked as such.
Dealing with false information: If an information is deemed to be false,
the following process needs to be applied:

 If the falsehood was:

    o  lack of information availability, mandatory correction is issued.
    o  misleading information: the source of misleading information is
         identified, correction is issued.
    o  ambiguity:  harm is examined,  correction issued
    o  coincidental: harm is examined, correction issued
    o  purposeful (a lie): harm is examined, legal action is explored


Universal access. Free press and press in general is granted access to
all possible events with no restrictions, except the following cases:

   o   Immediate danger or harm to the members of press or anyone involved
   o   Privacy considerations (underage protections ...)
   o   Legal considerations
   o   Case of national security violation

With access to events where a limited number of press can be accommodated,
the press as a unified entity should decide on access and sharing.
